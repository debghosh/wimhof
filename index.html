The Technique

The Wim Hof Method breathing technique is designed to increase energy, reduce stress levels, and augment the immune response.

Phase 1: Controlled Hyperventilation Take 30 power breaths. Inhale deeply through the nose or mouth (belly, then chest), and let the breath go unforced. Follow the blue circle.
Phase 2: Retention (The Hold) After the 30th exhale, stop breathing and hold. Hold for as long as you comfortably can. This resets your nervous system.
Phase 3: Recovery Breath When you feel the urge to breathe, take a deep breath in and hold for 15 seconds. Squeeze your head and chest gently to direct energy.
⚠️ Safety First: Never practice this near water, while driving, or standing up. You may faint. Practice sitting or lying down safely.
